A Literature Survey on Watermarking Techniques for AI Generated Text

Chandan R, Sreephaneesha K, Vivek G

Abstract—The rapid advancement of Large Language Models (LLMs) has significantly increased the volume of machine generated content across digital platforms. As AI-generated text becomes increasingly indistinguishable from human-written content, concerns regarding provenance, authenticity, misinformation, and accountability have emerged. Watermarking techniques have been proposed as a practical solution for identifying AI-generated text while preserving content quality. This paper presents a literature survey of five influential watermarking approaches for LLM-generated text. The surveyed methods include statistical token-level watermarking, robustness focused watermarking evaluation, production-scale watermark deployment, distortion-free watermarking, and publicly verifiable watermarking. The methodologies, advantages, limitations, and practical applications of each approach are analyzed. Furthermore, common research gaps and future research directions are identified to support the development of more robust and trustworthy watermarking frameworks for AI-generated content.

Index Terms—Large Language Models, Watermarking, AI Generated Text, Text Provenance, Content Authentication, Machine Generated Content

I. INTRODUCTION
Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed the field of natural language processing by enabling high-quality text generation across diverse domains such as education, software development, content creation, customer support, and research. While these systems offer substantial benefits, they also introduce challenges related to misinformation, plagiarism, impersonation, and content authenticity.
